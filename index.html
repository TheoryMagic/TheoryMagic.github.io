About

I study the foundations of reinforcement learning and bandit algorithms: regret analysis, optimal exploration, and inference-aware learning rules. I like theory that becomes practical algorithms and reproducible experiments. Applications sometimes involve networked or causal settings, but the emphasis is on clean theory and provable guarantees.

Research

My work centers on bandit theory and reinforcement learning theory. Recent results develop design-based bandit methods that balance learning efficiency (regret) with valid statistical inference. I also study robustness of RL systems—for example, when reward-poisoning can succeed in linear MDPs— and propose black-box evaluation protocols. The long-term goal is principled algorithms with clear guarantees and simple, reproducible implementations.

Keywords: RL theory · bandit theory · robustness · online learning · (applications to networks & causality when relevant).
