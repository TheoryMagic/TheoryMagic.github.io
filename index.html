About

I'm a Ph.D. student in Artificial Intelligence at Oregon State University. I work on the theory of reinforcement learning and bandit algorithms, with a focus on network interference, causal reasoning, and adversarial robustness. I enjoy turning theory into reproducible systems and experiments.

Previously: University of Science and Technology of China (USTC), School of the Gifted Young.

Research

I study design‑based bandits under interference, mapping the Pareto frontier between regret minimization and valid statistical inference. I am also interested in reward‑poisoning attackability in linear MDPs—characterizing when learning can be manipulated and developing practical black‑box evaluation protocols.

Keywords: regret analysis · exploration–exploitation · causal inference under interference · robustness · reproducibility.
