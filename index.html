About

I am a first‑year Ph.D. student in Artificial Intelligence at Oregon State University. My research focuses on the theory of reinforcement learning and bandit algorithms under network interference, causal assumptions, and adversarial robustness. I like building theory‑backed systems and reproducible experiments.

Previously: University of Science and Technology of China (USTC, School of the Gifted Young).

Research

My current work studies design‑based bandits under interference, exploring the Pareto frontier between regret minimization and valid statistical inference. I also investigate reward‑poisoning attackability in linear MDPs, seeking necessary and sufficient conditions and practical black‑box attack frameworks.

Keywords: regret analysis · exploration‑exploitation · causal inference under interference · robustness · reproducibility.
